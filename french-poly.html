The History of French Polynesia

French Polynesia is a collection of five tropical island groups midway between Australia and South America. The islands are: The Austral Islands, The Gambier Islands, The Marquesas Islands, The Society Islands, and the Tuamotu Archipelago.

The size of this country is actually less than ⅓ the size of Connecticut. In the 1830’s, Queen Pomare IV denied access to allow French Catholic missionaries to work, which led to the French-Tahitian War in the 1840’s.

In 1940, France granted island citizenship to the French and in 1957, the collection of islands were officially renamed “French Polynesia”.

The Environment of French Polynesia

Environment

In this country, the sea level rises to extreme measures. Droughts and fresh water scarcity could also occur.

Extreme weather conditions, like tsunamis, cyclones, storms, reef damage, erosion, and landslides could also occur in French Polynesia.

Climate

French Polynesia has a tropical climate.

Major Infectious Diseases

French Polynesia has a high risk of diseases. There are food or waterborne diseases, like bacterial diarrhea, or vector borne diseases, like malaria that one could catch in this country.
